# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Hobbyist restorers, vintage hi-fi collectors, bench techs, and first-time
fixers. They use SoundBench mid-repair: a tablet or laptop propped on a
workbench, hands occupied with a multimeter or soldering iron, often at
arm's length. Skill range is wide — from "owns a scope" to "never opened a
receiver" — and a skill-level setting adjusts instruction depth and which
repairs are recommended versus flagged "take it to a tech."

The job to be done: get a broken piece of vintage audio gear working again
without destroying it (or themselves), and keep a record of what worked.

## Product Purpose

An end-to-end repair companion for audio gear: pick a device, describe
symptoms, get a ranked diagnosis with confirming tests, follow guided repair
steps with the schematic's suspect section highlighted, and log the outcome
so the app learns which fixes actually work. Success looks like completed,
confirmed repairs — and a learning loop where community outcome data makes
every diagnosis sharper. (Full feature/architecture spec: PLAN.md.)

## Brand Personality

Warm, capable, trustworthy. Vintage flair earned from the gear itself — the
products are vintage, so the interface can carry period warmth — but the
flair lives in typography, color, and graphic language, never imitation
hardware.

Committed brand colors (Josh's choice, 2026-06-12): paper-warm background
`#f2f0e3`, near-black ink `#2e2e2e`, coral accent `#f76f53`. This replaces
the earlier dark-charcoal/amber direction seen in the first mockup round;
those mockups predate the pivot.

## Anti-references

- **Skeuomorphic retro pastiche.** No fake wood grain, no brushed-metal
  textures, no literal VU-meter widgets or knob controls. Vintage feel stays
  typographic and graphic, not imitation hardware.

## Design Principles

1. **Bench-first.** Designed for arm's-length tablet use mid-repair: large
   touch targets, glanceable steps, state that survives putting the device
   down for twenty minutes.
2. **Safety is interface, not fine print.** Mains, charged caps, and lasers
   get first-class UI moments — acknowledged, not scrolled past. Hazard cues
   never rely on color alone.
3. **Confidence with provenance.** Every diagnosis shows where it comes from:
   service manual, failure-mode database, or "confirmed by 14 community
   repairs on this model." No oracle answers.
4. **Meet the fixer at their level.** The same repair reads differently to a
   novice and a bench tech; depth adapts, and hazardous work is gated for
   beginners rather than hidden from experts.
5. **Vintage warmth, modern clarity.** Period character in the brand voice
   and graphic language; contemporary rigor in layout, hierarchy, and
   interaction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Bench-readable sizing is the named requirement: large touch targets and text
readable at arm's length under workshop lighting. Beyond that, sensible
defaults apply — body-text contrast ≥4.5:1 (deliberately generous given the
warm paper background), keyboard navigability, reduced-motion alternatives,
and safety warnings that pair icons and text rather than relying on color.
